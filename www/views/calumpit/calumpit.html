Calumpit

Calumpit was derived from the name of the hardwood trees called Calumpit, found abundantly growing in front of the Calumpit Catholic Church

History

Calumpit was already an established "barangay" even before the coming of the Spaniards.

Tourist Attractions

Calumpit River

"This river has for centuries shaped the lives of Bulakeños."

Meyto Shrine

"This is the cradle of Christianity in the province, where the first Catholic Mass was held by the Augustinian friars, armed with cross in 1572."

St. John the Baptist Church

"Built in 1572, the St. John the Baptist Church is the oldest church in Bulacan. Constructed under the supervision of Augustinian priest Fr. Diego Vivar-Ordoñez"

Bagbag Bridge

"The bridge now standing serves as a reminder of the valor displayed by the Filipino who triumphed against the American forces."
